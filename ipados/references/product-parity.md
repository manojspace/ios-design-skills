# Product Parity

The iPad app should feel exactly related to the iPhone app, not identical in geometry.

## Keep The Same

- Brand palette, icon language, typography roles, and tone.
- Primary product jobs and state model.
- Content/loading/empty/error/stale states.
- Signature product moments.
- Semantic motion language.
- Data formatting and terminology.

## Adapt For iPad

- Layout density and information architecture.
- Navigation depth: split view, sidebars, inspectors, and panels.
- Input methods: pointer, keyboard, Pencil, drag/drop.
- Multitasking and resizable windows.
- Menu bar and toolbar discoverability.
- More simultaneous context without making the screen noisy.

## Anti-Patterns

- Centering a narrow iPhone screen in a large canvas without a reason.
- Stretching cards to full width until scan lines are too long.
- Making every iPhone tab a sidebar item without reconsidering workflow.
- Hiding power actions that should be keyboard shortcuts, context menus, or toolbar/menu bar commands.
- Ignoring half-screen or Stage Manager windows.

## iPhone-To-iPad Translation

For every important iPhone screen, identify:

- Primary object: the thing the screen is about.
- Adjacent context: what users need beside it on iPad.
- Repeated action: what should be one click, shortcut, drag, or context menu.
- Comparison need: whether users need two items, days, documents, or states visible together.
- Editing depth: whether an inspector or detail panel replaces navigation pushes.
